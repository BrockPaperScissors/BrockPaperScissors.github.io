Project: Greenhouse

A cozy farming sim, or is it? Set in the big city where overpopulation was declared a decade ago, your land was reclaimed by the city to build residential units. Growing plants has become a somewhat rare hobby, but you manage to keep it alive in your very own kitchen.

  • Role: Project Lead & Lead Programmer
  • Submitted to the 2025 Spring Game Jam hosted by SMCs Corsair Game Jams club.
  • Built in the Godot engine and programmed in GDScript.
  • Led a team of 7 members who have never made a game before to push out a playable prototype in 3 weeks.
  • Managed collaboration efforts utilizing tools such as Jira, Miro, and Github.
  • Presented the game, our work flow, and gave an impromptu panel on the importance and application of version control when working on a project as part of a team.

Team Deets

A Match history tool made for League of Legends players. Users can enter their Riot ID and tag, and the app will utilize the Riot API's to display several statistics of each member in the users last 5 matches.

  • Created using ReactJS, utlizing React hooks, React Router, useState and useContext.
  • Leveraged external JSON data from several API's to create meaningful metrics that help players analyze their performance.

Baby Gandalph

Baby Gandalph is a browser-based game inspired by Tamagotchi where the player gives their character a name, their favorite food, and chooses a spell to specialize in. The goal is to level up their wizard by casting their chosen spell while managing their hunger and fatigue bars. If the player reaches a certain age before they are fully evolved, they get kicked out of the academy!

  • Built using HTML, CSS, Javascript/JQuery.
  • Employed OOP practices to create dynamic evolutions when the player reaches certain skill levels.
  • Learned a great deal about conditionally displayed content with modals and pop-up text boxes.

Build-A-Farm

Build-A-Farm was a project created and submitted to a 3-day hack-a-thon where the theme was accessibility. Our focus was on kids with learning disabilities and our goal was to create a math practice app that was lightweight, low-investment and quick to get started learning with.

  • Achieved a clean homepage layout using ReactJS, and utilized useState to create a dynamic progress bar for the game Cow Mania.
  • Implemented real-time feedback and the ability to try another answer if the users input was not correct.
  • Finished first place after presenting to the project to a panel of judges and accessibility experts.
